Artificial Intelligence Manifesto

Draft 0.1 • Last updated • Public consultation

Core Values (We value… over …)

  • Human judgment over algorithmic automation
  • Transparency over black-box efficiency
  • Augmentation over replacement
  • Ethical responsibility over unchecked innovation
  • Long-term trust over short-term gain

Guiding Principles

  1. AI is a tool, not a master. It amplifies human creativity, decision-making, and productivity.
  2. Fairness & accountability are non-negotiable. Measure, mitigate, and disclose risks and bias.
  3. Opportunity for all scales. Individuals, SMBs, and enterprises should benefit without widening gaps.
  4. Human connection matters. Design to enhance empathy, dignity, and community.
  5. Iterate responsibly. Pair innovation with governance, safety, and continuous learning.
  6. Data stewardship is a duty. Privacy, consent, security, and minimal collection by default.
  7. Open collaboration. Policymakers, businesses, academia, and the public co-create the path.
  8. Adaptive systems. Evolve responsibly as contexts and capabilities change.

Scopes of Application

  • Personal: Use AI to learn, create, and decide—own your inputs & outputs.
  • Small Business: Focus on productivity, customer value, and clear ROI with lightweight governance.
  • Enterprise: Formal risk management, model lifecycle oversight, and transparent vendor controls.
  • Society: Safety, rights, and equitable access as first-order concerns.
